Summary

The article argues that agent frameworks for large language models (LLMs) are unnecessary and often hinder progress, advocating instead for minimal, flexible agent designs that give models maximal freedom and only restrict as needed. It describes the evolution of the Browser Use agent, which relies on simple for-loops and direct browser control via Chrome DevTools Protocol and extension APIs, and highlights the importance of avoiding over-engineered abstractions. Practical implementation details, such as ephemeral messages and explicit task completion tools, are discussed to ensure robust and scalable agent behavior.
