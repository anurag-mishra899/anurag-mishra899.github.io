Summary

The post explains why high CPU usage alongside lower GPU utilization is common when serving large language models (LLMs) with vLLM. It highlights that while the GPU is compute-heavy during the "Prefill" phase, the "Decode" phase is often memory-bound, and the CPU is heavily involved in orchestration tasks such as scheduling, tokenization, and sampling, as well as handling Python and async overhead from frameworks like FastAPI or Uvicorn.
