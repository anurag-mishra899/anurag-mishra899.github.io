Summary

The article introduces nano-vLLM, a lightweight and minimal Python-based reimplementation of the vLLM inference engine for large language models, designed for speed, low memory usage, and hackability on consumer hardware. It highlights nano-vLLM's modular architecture, efficient caching, and support for features like Flash Attention and basic tensor parallelism, making it ideal for research, learning, and edge deployment.

The post explains why high CPU usage alongside lower GPU utilization is common during vLLM-based LLM inference. It highlights that while the GPU handles compute-heavy tasks during the "Prefill" phase, the "Decode" phase is often memory-bound, and the CPU is heavily involved in orchestration tasks such as scheduling, tokenization, and sampling, as well as handling Python and async overhead from frameworks like FastAPI.
