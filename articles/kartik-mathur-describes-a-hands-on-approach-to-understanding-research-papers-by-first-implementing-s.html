Summary

Kartik Mathur describes a hands-on approach to understanding research papers by first implementing simplified versions of their core algorithms using Claude Code, rather than starting with theoretical proofs. This method emphasizes building and observing toy models to make abstract concepts concrete and debuggable, leading to deeper comprehension before revisiting the paper’s mathematics. The post also shares resources and templates for replicating this workflow.
