Summary

The article discusses why high CPU utilization is common during vLLM-based LLM inference, even when GPU usage appears low. It explains that CPU handles orchestration tasks such as scheduling, tokenization, and sampling, while the GPU is not always the bottleneck, especially during memory-bound decode phases. Understanding these system-level behaviors is essential for optimizing infrastructure and throughput in LLM deployments.
