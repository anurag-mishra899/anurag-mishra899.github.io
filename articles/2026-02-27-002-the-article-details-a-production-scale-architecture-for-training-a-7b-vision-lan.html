Summary

The article details a production-scale architecture for training a 7B vision-language model using over 20TB of multimodal data streamed to 160 GPUs without downloading the entire dataset locally. It explains how MosaicML Streaming (MDS) enables high-throughput, fault-tolerant training by transforming cloud storage latency into efficient, shard-based data streaming, supported by decoupled tokenization, training, and validation pipelines. The approach maximizes GPU utilization and operational resilience for large-scale, multi-week training runs.
