Summary

The article explores the issue of GPU stragglers in large-scale distributed machine learning training, where a single slow GPU can significantly degrade cluster performance and increase costs. It emphasizes the need for cross-layer correlation of hardware and software signals to accurately diagnose and remediate straggler-induced slowdowns, using automated tools and workflows. A practical demo illustrates how infrastructure constraints on one GPU can impact the entire training job and highlights the challenges in identifying and resolving such issues.
