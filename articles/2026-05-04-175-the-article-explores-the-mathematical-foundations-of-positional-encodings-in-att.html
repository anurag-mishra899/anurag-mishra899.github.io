Summary

The article explores the mathematical foundations of positional encodings in attention mechanisms for language models, using group theory to rigorously characterize all possible encodings under reasonable constraints. It concludes that valid positional encodings must form a one-parameter group, showing that commonly used methods like RoPE exhaust the practical options, though some exotic alternatives exist in theory.
