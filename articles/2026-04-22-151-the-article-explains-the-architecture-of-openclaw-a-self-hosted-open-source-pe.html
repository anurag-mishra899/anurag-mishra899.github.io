Summary

The article explains the architecture of OpenClaw, a self-hosted, open-source personal AI assistant designed to feel autonomous by processing various event-driven inputs through a centralized control plane called the Gateway. It details how OpenClaw manages sessions, state, and concurrency, and clarifies that its apparent proactivity is a result of disciplined event handling rather than sentience.
