Summary

The post critiques the hype around vLLM for LLM inference, arguing that many influencers promote it without understanding inference engine internals. It explains that while vLLM excels at high-concurrency, single-turn workloads, SGLang is significantly faster for agentic, multi-turn, or prefix-reuse scenarios due to its RadixAttention caching. The author provides a practical decision matrix for choosing between inference engines based on workload characteristics.
