The post explains why high CPU usage alongside lower GPU utilization is common during vLLM-based LLM inference. It highlights that while the GPU handles compute-heavy tasks during the "Prefill" phase, the "Decode" phase is often memory-bound, and the CPU is heavily involved in orchestration tasks such as scheduling, tokenization, and sampling, as well as handling Python and async overhead from frameworks like FastAPI. Understanding these behaviors is crucial for optimizing infrastructure and throughput in LLM deployments.
