The article argues that agent frameworks for large language models (LLMs) are unnecessary and often

Web Article 📅 2026-04-24 👤 Shared by Anurag Mishra 🏷️ Uncategorized
📎 Original Source:
https://browser-use.com/posts/bitter-lesson-agent-frameworks

Summary

The article argues that agent frameworks for large language models (LLMs) are unnecessary and often hinder progress, advocating for minimal abstractions that give models maximal freedom to act. It describes the design philosophy behind the BU Agent, which exposes raw browser control surfaces and relies on simple, explicit mechanisms for task completion and context management. The author emphasizes that most intelligence and capability reside within the model itself, and that over-engineering frameworks constrains learning and adaptability.
