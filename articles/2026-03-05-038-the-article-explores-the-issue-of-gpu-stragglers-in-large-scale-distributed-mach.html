Summary

The article explores the issue of GPU stragglers in large-scale distributed machine learning training, where a single slow GPU can significantly degrade overall system performance and waste substantial compute resources. It emphasizes that straggler problems are often rooted in hardware and infrastructure issues, requiring cross-layer correlation and automated tools for effective detection and remediation. The article also demonstrates a practical example of identifying and resolving a compute straggler using monitoring and correlation dashboards.
