Summary

The article explains the architecture of OpenClaw, a self-hosted, open-source AI assistant designed to feel autonomous by processing multiple event-driven inputs through a centralized control plane (Gateway), session management, and a disciplined event loop. It details how OpenClaw routes events, manages session state, prevents concurrency issues, and maintains security, emphasizing that its "autonomy" is an engineered illusion rather than true sentience.
